The Kabini River Lodge

Originally built in the 1800s, Kabini River Resort, also known as Kabini River Lodge, was once the private hunting retreat of the Mysore Maharaja. Today, it is India's first eco-tourism venture, offering travelers a fantastic opportunity to bond with nature and the wild.

Located within the Nagarhole Tiger Reserve, this 54-acre lodge has been voted one of the top five wildlife resorts worldwide by Tatler magazine. Here, you're not just near nature; you're fully embraced by it, allowing you to connect and unwind.

Journey into Kabini

Encounter with Elephants

Kabini is home to the largest congregation of Asian elephants in the wild, often numbering over 300 in peak season. With guided safaris, boat rides on the river, and expert-led nature walks, we bring you face-to-face with the wonders of the Kabini ecosystem. Stay with us if you're ready for an adventure steeped in history and natural beauty. The wild is waiting!
